Core Topics
Study all sections below

🧠 The Brain — Major Regions

  • Cerebrum: Largest part. Thinking, feeling, moving, language, memory. Divided into 4 lobes.
  • Frontal lobe: Planning, movement, personality, speech (Broca's area)
  • Parietal lobe: Sensory processing, spatial awareness
  • Temporal lobe: Hearing, memory, language comprehension (Wernicke's area)
  • Occipital lobe: Vision processing
  • Cerebellum: Balance, coordination, posture
  • Brain stem: Breathing, heart rate, sleep-wake cycles, connects brain to spinal cord

🔌 Neurons & Impulses

  • Neuron: Basic unit of the nervous system — transmits electrical signals
  • Dendrites: Receive signals from other neurons
  • Cell body (soma): Contains the nucleus; processes signals
  • Axon: Sends signals away from cell body
  • Myelin sheath: Fatty coating that speeds signal transmission
  • Synapse: Gap between neurons; signals cross via neurotransmitters
  • Action potential: Electrical impulse traveling along the axon

🌐 CNS vs PNS

  • CNS (Central Nervous System): Brain + Spinal Cord
  • PNS (Peripheral Nervous System): All nerves outside brain/spinal cord
  • Sensory (afferent) nerves: Carry signals TO the CNS
  • Motor (efferent) nerves: Carry signals FROM the CNS to muscles/glands
  • Somatic NS: Voluntary muscle control
  • Autonomic NS: Involuntary — controls organs, glands, heart rate
  • Sympathetic: Fight-or-flight response
  • Parasympathetic: Rest-and-digest response

👁️ Special Senses

  • Sight: Retina (rods = dim light; cones = color) → optic nerve → occipital lobe
  • Hearing: Eardrum → ossicles (hammer, anvil, stirrup) → cochlea → auditory nerve
  • Smell: Olfactory receptors in nasal epithelium → olfactory nerve → brain
  • Taste: Taste buds on tongue (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami)
  • Balance: Semicircular canals and vestibule in inner ear
  • Proprioception: Sense of body position from muscles and joints

💊 Drugs & the Nervous System

  • Stimulants: Speed up CNS activity (caffeine, amphetamines)
  • Depressants: Slow down CNS activity (alcohol, barbiturates)
  • Hallucinogens: Alter perception (LSD, psilocybin)
  • Opioids: Block pain signals, highly addictive (morphine, heroin)
  • Drugs affect neurotransmitters — they can mimic, block, or flood receptors
  • Long-term drug use can cause permanent changes to brain structure

🏛️ Other Brain Structures

  • Thalamus: Relay station — routes sensory info to correct brain area
  • Hypothalamus: Regulates temperature, hunger, thirst, hormones
  • Amygdala: Processes emotions, especially fear
  • Hippocampus: Long-term memory formation and retrieval
  • Corpus callosum: Band of fibers connecting left & right cerebral hemispheres
  • Pituitary gland: Master endocrine gland controlled by hypothalamus
  • Basal ganglia: Movement coordination and habit formation
Key Vocabulary
Know every term before test day
TermDefinition
NeuronThe basic functional unit of the nervous system; a nerve cell that transmits electrical impulses.
DendritesBranch-like extensions of a neuron that receive incoming signals from other neurons.
AxonThe long fiber of a neuron that carries electrical impulses away from the cell body.
Myelin sheathFatty coating around an axon that insulates it and speeds signal conduction.
SynapseThe junction between two neurons where neurotransmitters carry signals across a gap.
NeurotransmitterChemical messenger released at a synapse to transmit signals between neurons.
Action potentialAn electrical impulse that travels along the axon when the neuron fires.
CNSCentral Nervous System — the brain and spinal cord.
PNSPeripheral Nervous System — all nerves outside the brain and spinal cord.
Somatic nervous systemDivision of PNS controlling voluntary skeletal muscle movement.
Autonomic nervous systemDivision of PNS controlling involuntary functions: heart, digestion, glands.
Sympathetic NSAutonomic division that triggers fight-or-flight response (increases heart rate, dilates pupils).
Parasympathetic NSAutonomic division that promotes rest-and-digest (slows heart rate, aids digestion).
CerebrumLargest brain region — responsible for thinking, movement, sensation, language, and memory.
CerebellumBrain region below the cerebrum that coordinates balance, posture, and smooth movement.
Brain stemConnects brain to spinal cord; controls breathing, heart rate, and other vital functions.
ThalamusBrain relay station that routes sensory information to appropriate cortical areas.
HypothalamusRegulates body temperature, hunger, thirst, and hormonal output from the pituitary gland.
HippocampusCritical for forming and retrieving long-term memories; located in temporal lobe.
AmygdalaProcesses emotions especially fear and threat; part of the limbic system.
ProprioceptionThe sense of body position and movement, detected by receptors in muscles and joints.
Reflex arcA neural pathway that bypasses the brain for rapid responses to stimuli (e.g., knee jerk).
