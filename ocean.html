What to Study
Core content areas for the Ocean Science written event

Note on Test Topics

Division A does not release previous tests. Topics below are drawn from the official VA Division A Rules Manual study guide and Olympiad-level slides. Use these as a starting framework and research further.

🌍 The 5 Oceans

  • Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, Arctic
  • Size order and key facts
  • 71% of Earth's surface
  • Average depth: 3,688 m

🌊 Ocean Zones

  • Epipelagic (Sunlight, 0–200m)
  • Mesopelagic (Twilight, 200–1,000m)
  • Bathypelagic (Midnight, 1,000–4,000m)
  • Abyssopelagic + Hadalpelagic

🌀 Currents

  • Thermohaline circulation
  • Surface vs. deep currents
  • Coriolis Effect
  • Gulf Stream and upwelling

🧪 Ocean Chemistry

  • Salinity: avg 35 per mille
  • Ocean pH and acidification
  • CO2 absorption (~30%)
  • Carbonic acid formation

✨ Bioluminescence

  • Luciferin + luciferase + O2
  • Why animals glow (5 reasons)
  • ~76% of deep-sea animals
  • Blue-green light travels furthest

🌙 Tides

  • Moon's gravitational pull
  • Spring vs. neap tides
  • Bay of Fundy: ~16m tidal range
  • Intertidal zone ecology

🐾 Food Webs

  • 5 trophic levels
  • 10% Rule for energy transfer
  • Keystone species (sea otters)
  • Marine snow, trophic cascade

🪸 Coral Reefs

  • Zooxanthellae (symbiotic algae)
  • Coral bleaching causes
  • Reef types: fringing, barrier, atoll
  • Less than 1% ocean floor, 25% of species

🌡️ Climate & Oceans

  • Ocean absorbs 30% human CO2
  • Acidification effects on coral
  • El Nino / La Nina (ENSO)
  • Sea level rise: thermal expansion

🌋 Ocean Floor

  • Mid-ocean ridges (divergent)
  • Trenches (subduction/convergent)
  • Hydrothermal vents + chemosynthesis
  • Mariana Trench: ~11,034 m

🦑 Deep-Sea Animals

  • Anglerfish, giant squid, barreleye
  • Adaptations to pressure and darkness
  • Vampire squid (Vampyromorphida)
  • Dumbo octopus (deepest: 6,957m)

🔬 Ocean Exploration

  • HMS Challenger (1872-1876)
  • Trieste dive 1960, Mariana Trench
  • Hydrothermal vents found 1977
  • Only ~20% of ocean mapped

Key Vocabulary
Know every term below before test day
TermDefinition
SalinityConcentration of dissolved salts, measured in parts per thousand (ppt or ‰). Ocean average = 35‰.
ThermoclineLayer in the ocean where temperature drops rapidly with increasing depth.
Thermohaline CirculationGlobal deep ocean current driven by density differences from temperature and salinity — the ocean conveyor belt.
Coriolis EffectDeflection of ocean currents to the right in the N. hemisphere and left in the S. hemisphere due to Earth's rotation.
UpwellingCold, nutrient-rich deep water rising to the ocean surface, fueling phytoplankton and fisheries.
ENSOEl Niño Southern Oscillation — alternating warm (El Niño) and cool (La Niña) Pacific phases affecting global weather.
EpipelagicSunlight Zone (0–200m). Photosynthesis occurs. Most ocean life concentrated here.
BioluminescenceLight produced by a living organism through a chemical reaction (luciferin + luciferase + oxygen).
LuciferinThe light-producing molecule (substrate) in bioluminescent reactions.
LuciferaseThe enzyme that triggers the bioluminescent reaction, causing luciferin to emit light.
Spring TideLargest tidal range — occurs when Sun, Earth, and Moon are aligned (new or full moon).
Neap TideSmaller tidal range — occurs during quarter moons when Sun and Moon are at 90 degrees to Earth.
PhytoplanktonMicroscopic photosynthetic marine organisms at the base of most ocean food webs. Produce ~50% of Earth's oxygen.
10% RuleOnly ~10% of energy passes from one trophic level to the next. 90% is lost as heat and waste.
Keystone SpeciesA species whose presence has a disproportionately large effect on its ecosystem. Removal causes major change.
Marine SnowDead organic matter and microbes that drift slowly from surface to deep ocean, feeding deep-sea life.
SubductionWhen one tectonic plate dives beneath another at a convergent boundary, forming ocean trenches.
ChemosynthesisProduction of food using chemical energy, not sunlight. Performed by bacteria at hydrothermal vents.
ZooxanthellaeSymbiotic photosynthetic algae living inside coral polyps, providing up to 90% of their energy.
Ocean AcidificationDecrease in ocean pH caused by absorption of CO2, forming carbonic acid. pH is now ~8.1 (was 8.25).
Hadal ZoneDeepest ocean zone, below 6,000m. Found only in ocean trenches. Extreme pressure and near-freezing.
Trophic LevelA feeding level in a food chain — producers at Level 1, apex predators at Level 5.
