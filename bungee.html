🥚 Bungee Egg Drop

Design a rubber band bungee system to protect a raw egg — dropping from a two-story stairwell and stopping as close as possible to the Artificial Horizon without going past it.

Grades 3–4 45-minute session 2 students per team
Event Overview
Official description from the VA Division A Rules Manual

📋 The Challenge

Design and construct a bungee cord system using rubber bands to protect a raw egg sealed in a plastic bag. The egg is dropped from a two-story stairwell railing. Goal: stop the egg as close as possible to the Artificial Horizon (zero line) without going past it. Score = Assessment + Best Drop Score (max 100 points).

⚠️ Safety — Required to Compete

  • ANSI Z87 D3 Splash/Droplet standard goggles (full seal) must be worn during ALL event testing
  • Must show "D3" on the label — regular impact glasses are not sufficient
  • All competitors wear goggles during any team's drop — not just your own
  • Without proper goggles = no-show score

🚫 Key Restrictions

  • Tape may ONLY be used to attach the bungee cord to the plastic bag
  • Tape may NOT support or strengthen the egg or the bag itself
  • Egg must be sealed inside the provided plastic bag
  • Any visible egg leakage = broken = Tier 2 violation (−20%)

📝 Written Assessment (simultaneous with build)

  • Taken SIMULTANEOUSLY during the 25-minute build phase
  • Topics: Law of Conservation of Energy, Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE), Kinetic Energy (KE), Elastic Potential Energy (EPE)
  • Also state your estimated drop distance on the assessment paper — this is Tiebreaker 1!

★ Worked Example

TDD=400cm. Your RAS=6, best=9 → AS=33.33. 1st drop below horizon → DS1=25.45. 2nd drop 13cm from horizon → DS2=49.24. FS = 33.33 + 49.24 = 82.57 points.

Tier Violations

✅ Tier 1 — Full Score

Device meets all building requirements. Egg remains unbroken (no leakage). Team follows Spirit of the Problem guidelines.

⚠️ Tier 2 — Score × 0.80 (−20%)

Device has a construction or timing fault violation. Team otherwise follows Spirit of the Problem.

❌ Tier 3 — Score × 0.70 (−30%)

Team did not follow Spirit of the Problem guidelines.

Build Strategy
Recommended approach for the 45-minute session
1

Listen for TDD announcement

Minute 0

The Event Supervisor announces the Target Drop Distance (TDD) — distance from railing to Artificial Horizon. Minimum = 200 cm. Write it down immediately.

  • Assign roles: one builds the bungee, one takes written test
  • Both must know the TDD to calculate bungee length needed
2

Choose and connect rubber bands

Minutes 1–15

Select bands to build your bungee cord. Use the TDD to estimate needed stretch. Bands in series (end-to-end) = longer. Bands in parallel (side-by-side) = stiffer.

  • #19 = stiffest/shortest • #117B = longest/softest
  • Mix sizes to fine-tune stopping distance
  • You may cut bands to adjust stiffness
3

Seal egg in plastic bag

Minutes 15–20

Choose a room-temperature egg from those provided. Seal it completely inside the plastic bag. Any egg leakage after the drop = broken = Tier 2.

  • Practice sealing quickly — you do this under time pressure
  • Attach bungee cord to the bag: knot, tape, or both
  • Tape may ONLY attach cord to bag — not touch egg or reinforce bag
4

Announce your estimate

Before drop

State your estimated drop distance to the Event Supervisor before the drop. This is Tiebreaker 1 — be as precise as possible based on your bungee calculations.

  • Write the same estimate on your assessment paper
  • A precise estimate shows understanding of the physics
5

Competition drops

Drop phase

Attach bungee to Retaining Hook. Hold egg vertical at platform edge. Count "3, 2, 1, drop" and OPEN HAND — do not push.

  • DO NOT push the egg — you may not move it upward while releasing
  • Supervisor records slow-motion video of each drop
6

Adjust between drops

Between drops

You receive two drops. Use drop 1 as data. Adjust bungee before drop 2.

  • Egg went past horizon? Add shorter/stiffer bands or cut one shorter
  • Egg stopped too high? Add longer/softer bands or remove a short one
  • Your BEST drop score is used — not the average
Competition Tips
What wins — and what to avoid
🎯

The Artificial Horizon is the target

The zero line is the Artificial Horizon. Stopping as close to zero as possible — without going past — earns the highest drop score. Going below zero = DD = 200cm = worst possible drop score.

🔴

D3 goggles — not just glasses

Full-seal ANSI Z87 D3 (splash-rated) goggles are required. Regular impact glasses are not sufficient. The label must say "D3". Without proper goggles you cannot compete.

📏

Calibrate before you finalize

Test your bungee on the floor first: attach to a fixed point, hold the bagged egg up, let it hang. Measure total stretch vs. TDD. Adjust before you declare your design done.

✂️

Cutting bands = fine tuning

Cutting a rubber band makes it shorter and effectively stiffer. A small cut can be the difference between stopping at the horizon and going past it. Make small adjustments between drops.

⚡

Study the energy formulas

Assessment = 50 points. Know GPE = mgh, KE = ½mv², EPE = ½kx². Know the full energy transformation: GPE → KE → EPE → KE → GPE. This is tested while you build.

🛡️

Seal the egg completely

Seal the egg completely in the plastic bag before every drop. Any leakage after the drop = broken = Tier 2 (−20% of your total score). Practice sealing quickly and securely.
