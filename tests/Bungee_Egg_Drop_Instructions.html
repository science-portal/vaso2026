Bungee Egg Drop

VA Division A. Design a rubber band bungee cord system to protect a raw egg — dropping from a two-story stairwell and stopping as close as possible to the Artificial Horizon.

Grades 3–4 45-Minute Session 2 Students Per Team

🎯 Event Overview
FROM THE OFFICIAL 2025–2026 VA DIVISION A RULES MANUAL

🥚 The Challenge

Design and construct a bungee cord system using rubber bands to protect a raw egg from breaking when it jumps from the top of a two-story stairwell. The goal is to stop the egg as close as possible to the Artificial Horizon (a zero-line on a chart) — without going past it.

Score = Assessment Score (50 pts) + Best Drop Score (50 pts) = max 100

⚠️ Safety — REQUIRED

  • ANSI Z87 D3 Splash/Droplet standard goggles must be worn during ALL event testing
  • This is a D3 rating (splash protection) — different from standard impact glasses
  • All competitors must wear them — even during other teams' drops
  • Without proper goggles = no-show score

📝 Written Assessment Topics

The written test covers: Law of Conservation of Energy, Elastic Potential Energy (EPE), Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE), and Kinetic Energy (KE). This test is answered simultaneously during the 25-minute build phase.

🏅 Official Scoring
EXACT FORMULA FROM THE RULES MANUAL

📊 Score Formula

FS = Assessment Score (AS) + max(Drop Score 1, Drop Score 2)

• AS = 50 × (your Raw Assessment Score ÷ best RAS among all teams)

• DS = 50 × (TDD − your DD) ÷ max(TDD − DD for all teams)

• If egg drops BELOW the horizon: DD = 200 cm (maximum penalty distance)

Example: TDD=400cm. Your RAS=6, best=9 → AS=33.33. Your 2nd drop DD=13cm → DS2=(400−13)÷(400−7)×50=49.24. FS=82.57.

🪂 Competition Phase — Step by Step
EXACT PROCEDURE FROM THE OFFICIAL RULES
1

Attach Bungee to Retaining Hook

Attach the END of your bungee cord opposite the egg to the Retaining Hook on the Bungee Jump Platform. Make sure the rest of the rubber bands are hanging loose over the front of the platform.

2

Position the Egg

Place and hold the egg (in the bag) at the end of the jump platform in a VERTICAL position. You may hold the lander in any manner as long as the bungee is attached to the hook and the egg is at the platform edge.

3

Announce Ready — Countdown

Announce to the Event Supervisor that you are ready. Wait for their reply that the camera is ready. Then count down from 3: "3, 2, 1, drop" and OPEN YOUR HAND. DO NOT PUSH THE EGG.

4

Video Recording

The Event Supervisor records the drop in slow motion showing the entire Artificial Horizon Chart (−100cm to +100cm). The lowest point of the egg is measured to the nearest 1cm mark from the video.

5

Adjust Between Drops

You receive two drops. After the first drop, you may adjust your bungee cord. Use your first drop as data: did the egg stop too high (add longer bands) or go too far (add shorter/stiffer bands)?

6

Egg Inspection

After the drops, the egg is removed from the plastic bag by the team and rolled on a paper towel. Any wet areas on the towel indicate leakage — egg is deemed broken (Tier 2 violation). Cracks without leaking = NOT broken.

⚡ Strategy Tips
HOW TO DESIGN YOUR BUNGEE SYSTEM
📏

Test on the Floor First

Before the competition, practice: attach your bungee to a fixed point, hold the bagged egg at the top, measure how far it stretches. Compare to the TDD — you want the egg to stop AT the horizon, not past it.

🔴

Band Size Matters

#19 = stiffest/shortest. #117B = longest/softest. Bands in series (end-to-end) = longer, more stretch. Bands in parallel (side-by-side) = stiffer. Mix sizes to fine-tune your stopping distance.

✂️

Cut Bands to Calibrate

Cutting a rubber band makes it shorter and effectively stiffer. A small cut can be the difference between just reaching the horizon and going past it. Make small adjustments between drops.

📊

Accurate Estimate = Tiebreaker Win

If tied, the team with the closest estimate to their actual drop distance wins. Time your test drop carefully and give an honest, precise estimate — not a guess.

⚡

Study the Energy Formulas

GPE = mgh, KE = ½mv², EPE = ½kx². Know how energy transforms: GPE → KE → EPE → KE → GPE (minus losses). This is 50 points of your score.

🛡️

Egg Safety = Tier 1

A broken egg = Tier 2 (−20% of total score). Always seal the egg completely in the plastic bag. The bag catches any leakage — critical for keeping your Tier 1 status.
