Astronaut Lander

VA Division A. Build a landing device that protects a marshmallow astronaut dropped from a two-story stairwell. Score = Assessment + Flight Time. Keep it in the air as long as possible!

🎯 Event Overview
FROM THE OFFICIAL 2025–2026 VA DIVISION A RULES MANUAL

🚀 The Challenge

Build a landing device that will safely protect a simulated astronaut (a regular marshmallow inside the provided paper cup capsule) from injury when landing from a two-story stairwell. Success = marshmallow stays in the cup. Score = flight time + written assessment.

Score = Assessment Score (50 pts) + Time Score (50 pts) = max 100

⚠️ Safety — REQUIRED

  • ANSI Z87+ impact-rated safety glasses must be worn during any competitor's flight phase
  • ALL competitors must wear eye protection — not just the team dropping
  • Eyeglasses are NOT safety rated — do not substitute them
  • No proper eye protection = no-show score
  • Teams must bring their own safety glasses

📝 Written Assessment Topics

The written test covers: forces and factors experienced by an astronaut when landing back on Earth after a stay in space — gravity, air resistance, impact forces, g-forces, microgravity effects on the human body, and the challenges of re-entry.

🏅 Official Scoring
EXACT FORMULA FROM THE RULES MANUAL

📊 Score Formula

Final Score (FS) = Assessment Score (TAS) + Time Score (TTS). Max = 100 points.

• TAS = (your assessment score ÷ best assessment score among all teams) × 50

• TTS = (your flight time ÷ longest flight time among all teams) × 50

Example: CAS=15, team scores 9 → TAS=(9÷15)×50=30. CFS=3.56 sec, team time=1.45 → TTS=(1.45÷3.56)×50=20.4. Final Score=50.4 points.

🪂 Launch Phase — Exact Procedure
OFFICIAL STEPS FROM THE RULES MANUAL
1

Place Marshmallow in Cup

Place the regular marshmallow in the provided paper cup. Success is defined as the marshmallow NOT exiting the capsule when landing. It may NOT be wedged or taped in.

2

Hold Lander at the Railing

Hold the lander so the bottom of the lander is parallel to the safety railing. You may hold it in any manner as long as this position is maintained.

3

Announce Ready

Announce to the Event Supervisor that you are ready to drop. Wait for a reply from the supervisor.

4

4-Point Countdown

Use a 4-point countdown such as "3, 2, 1, drop" to start the lander falling. You may NOT move the lander UPWARD while letting go.

5

Flight Time Recorded

The Event Supervisor and a judge measure and record the time. Time continues if the lander bounces off an object — it stops when the lander gets stuck or comes to a complete stop. Recorded to 1/100 second.

6

Inspect the Marshmallow

After landing, check whether the marshmallow is still in the cup. Marshmallow out = Tier 2 (−20%). Injury is defined as the marshmallow being crushed or significantly deformed.

⚡ Design Tips
HOW TO MAXIMIZE FLIGHT TIME AND KEEP THE MARSHMALLOW IN
☕

Use the #6 Coffee Filter

The #6 filter is larger than the #2. Larger area = more air drag = slower descent = longer flight time. Attach string lines from 4+ evenly-spaced points around the filter edge to the cup/frame.

⚖️

Keep It Light

Every extra gram increases fall speed. Use minimum materials to achieve maximum drag. The coffee filters are already very lightweight parachute material — don't add unnecessary weight.

🥛

Cup Must Stay Upright

The cup opening must face up throughout the descent — otherwise the marshmallow will fall out before landing. Test this by holding your lander by the parachute and letting it hang freely — is the cup upright?

📐

Respect the 10cm Rule

Nothing built within 10cm directly above the cup lip. Plan your parachute attachment so strings or structures connect to the SIDES of the cup or to a frame below the 10cm zone, not crossing over the top opening.

⏱️

Time Your Test Drop

Practice dropping from a safe height before competition. Time it with a stopwatch. Give an accurate estimate — tiebreaker 1 is the team whose estimated time is closest to actual. Don't just guess!

📝

Study Both Parts Equally

Assessment = 50 pts. Know: gravity, air resistance, impact force, g-forces, what happens to bones/muscles/heart in space, and what astronauts experience when returning to Earth.
