📋 The Challenge

Teams will build a strong, stable tower using paper straws and masking tape only to hold a mass of 300 grams. They will also answer questions on a written assessment on the types and building of towers.

Score = Assessment Score (50 pts) + Height Score (50 pts) = max 100

  • Eyeglasses are NOT safety rated — do not substitute them
  • Teams must bring their own safety glasses — they are not provided
  • No proper eye protection = no-show score (no exceptions)

🏅 Official Scoring
EXACT FORMULA FROM THE RULES MANUAL

📊 Score Formula

Final Score (FS) = Assessment Score (TAS) + Height Score (THS). Maximum = 100 points.

• Assessment: TAS = (your score ÷ best score among all teams) × 50

• Height: THS = (your height in mm ÷ tallest height among all teams) × 50

Example: Best height = 450mm, your height = 375mm → THS = (375÷450)×50 = 41.67 pts. Best assessment = 9, you score 4 → TAS = (4÷9)×50 = 22.22 pts. Final = 63.89 points.

🔧 Build Strategy
RECOMMENDED APPROACH FOR THE 45-MINUTE SESSION
1

Assign Roles Immediately

One student takes the written test. One student leads the build. Both must know the plan before the clock starts. The test and build happen in the SAME session — divide and conquer.

2

Design Before Building (5 min)

Sketch your tower design. Decide: square base or triangular? How many tiers? Where will the 5×5cm top platform be? Count your straws — allocate: ~6 for base, ~18 for upper tiers, ~4 for diagonals, 2 reserve.

3

Build Base First — Triangulate Immediately

Build the bottom tier and add diagonal braces before going up. A square base with no diagonals will collapse sideways. Add at least one diagonal on every rectangular face before moving upward.

  • Aim for base at least 10×10 cm for stability
  • Use tape to bundle 2–3 straws at the base corners for extra compression strength
4

Build in Tiers — Brace Each Level

Add height in stages of ~15 cm. Brace each tier before building the next. Check vertical alignment after every tier. A 5° lean at the bottom becomes a catastrophic lean at the top.

5

Build the Top Platform

The 5×5cm plywood block must sit flat on your tower top. Tape 2–3 straws in a flat cross pattern at the top to create a stable, level platform wide enough to hold the block securely.

6

Use Nearly All Straws

Unused straws count against you in tiebreakers. Add final diagonal braces or reinforce weak-looking joints with any remaining straws. The goal is to use as many as possible while keeping the tower strong.

⚡ Engineering Tips
WHAT WINS TOWER BUILDING COMPETITIONS
🔺

Triangulate Everything

Every rectangular frame MUST have a diagonal. A single diagonal straw converts a collapsible rectangle into two rigid triangles. This is the most important rule in the event.

📏

Short Columns, Not Long

Halving column height makes it ~4× more resistant to buckling. Keep unsupported straw lengths to 15cm or less. Add lateral braces at mid-height for tall sections.

🩹

Wrap Tape, Don't Just Stick It

Wrapping tape around a joint creates a compression band — far stronger than tape that just covers the surface. Overlap straws 2–3 cm before taping for maximum strength.

⬇️

Wide Base, Narrow Top

A pyramidal shape keeps the center of gravity low and the base footprint wide — making your tower much harder to tip over when the 300g mass is placed on top.

📐

Check Vertical Constantly

Hold a straw or pencil next to your tower to check it's plumb (straight). A lean that looks small at 20cm looks disqualifying at 50cm. Fix it while it's easy — not later.

📝

Study Both Parts Equally

The assessment and height are each worth 50 points. A 10cm shorter tower can be overcome by a perfect assessment score. Study tower types, stability, and key structures!
